## Version 1.1


## Purpose

By making a contribution to this project, I certify that:

1. The contribution was created in whole or in part by me and I have the right to submit it under the open source license indicated in the file; or

2. The contribution is based upon previous work that, to the best of my knowledge, is covered under an appropriate open source license and I have the right under that license to submit that work with modifications, whether created in whole or in part by me, under the same open source license (unless I am permitted to submit under a different license), as indicated in the file; or

3. The contribution was provided directly to me by some other person who certified (1), (2) or (3) and I have not modified it.

4. I understand and agree that this project and the contribution are public and that a record of the contribution (including all personal information I submit with it, including my sign-off) is maintained indefinitely and may be redistributed consistent with this project or the open source license(s) involved.

## How to Sign Off

To sign off a contribution, add the following line to your commit message:

```
Signed-off-by: Your Name <your.email@example.com>
```

By adding this line, you certify that you have read and agreed to the DCO above.

### Git Sign-Off Command

You can use `git commit -s` to automatically append your sign-off to the commit message.

For pull requests made through the GitHub web interface, add a comment or include the `Signed-off-by` trailer in the PR description.

### Example

```
feat: add IEEE template support

Implements the IEEE journal formatting template with proper section
headings and citation styling.

Signed-off-by: Jane Doe <jane.doe@example.com>
```

## Why DCO?

The DCO is a lightweight, per-commit certification mechanism used by the Linux Kernel and thousands of open source projects. It ensures that every contributor has the legal right to submit their work, without requiring a separate Contributor License Agreement (CLA) or copyright assignment.

A DCO is the preferred model for projects that want to keep the contribution process simple while maintaining legal traceability.
